Top NFL Quarterbacks: Best of All Time Revealed

Who is the greatest NFL quarterback of all time? It’s a question that sparks intense debates and passionate arguments among football fans. As the central figure in a team’s success, the quarterback’s impact on the game cannot be underestimated. So, who are the top NFL quarterbacks in history? Which players have left an indelible mark on the sport? Let’s dive into the fascinating world of the best quarterbacks the NFL has ever seen.

Matt Ryan: A Franchise-Altering Quarterback

Matt Ryan, quarterback for the Atlanta Falcons, has had a franchise-altering start in his career. While his team’s Super Bowl loss often garners attention, Ryan’s accomplishments cannot be overlooked. He has solidified himself as one of the top quarterbacks in the NFL, earning four Pro Bowl selections and leading the Falcons to a Super Bowl appearance.

In 2016, Ryan had a standout season and was named the NFL MVP. He showcased his exceptional skills by throwing for over 4,900 yards and 38 touchdowns, leading the Falcons’ high-powered offense. Ryan’s ability to read defenses and make precise throws has made him a formidable opponent for any team.

“I think Matt Ryan is a fantastic football player. There’s a reason why he’s an MVP. There’s a reason why they’ve had the success that they’ve had over the years.” – Sean McVay, Los Angeles Rams Head Coach

Ryan’s impact on the Atlanta Falcons franchise has been significant. After the departure of Michael Vick, the team was in need of a leader to restore relevance. Ryan stepped up to the challenge and has brought consistency and success to the organization. With his exceptional play on the field and his leadership off the field, he has become the face of the franchise.

    Norm Van Brocklin: Lighting Up the NFL

    In the early days of the forward pass, Norm Van Brocklin emerged as a trailblazing quarterback who left an indelible mark on the game. Known for his exceptional passing skills and strategic prowess, Van Brocklin’s impact was felt throughout his career.

    One of Van Brocklin’s most notable accomplishments is his record for the most passing yards in a single game. On September 28, 1951, while playing for the Los Angeles Rams, he threw for a remarkable 554 yards, a record that still stands to this day. This extraordinary achievement showcased his ability to dominate the field and set new standards for the position.

    Van Brocklin’s time with the Los Angeles Rams was highlighted by his leadership and excellence in guiding the team to their only championship until the 2020s. His talent and determination propelled the Rams to victory in the 1951 NFL title game, solidifying his place in football history.

    In 1958, Van Brocklin joined the Philadelphia Eagles and continued to impress with his exceptional play. Under his guidance, the Eagles went on to win the 1960 NFL Championship, a significant accomplishment that cemented Van Brocklin’s legacy as a top-tier quarterback.

    Recognized for his outstanding performances, Van Brocklin earned nine Pro Bowl selections and was named NFL MVP in 1961. His contributions to the game extended far beyond individual accolades, as he revolutionized the quarterback position and set a standard of excellence for future generations.

    The Legacy Lives On

    Norm Van Brocklin’s impact as a quarterback can still be felt in the NFL today. His passing prowess and record-breaking achievements continue to inspire and awe football enthusiasts. From his remarkable 554-yard passing performance to leading the Rams and Eagles to championships, Van Brocklin’s legacy as a true legend of the game remains intact.

    “Norm Van Brocklin’s record-breaking passing yards in a single game epitomizes his skill and influence on the game. He paved the way for future generations of quarterbacks and left an indelible mark on NFL history.” – Football Historian

    Ben Roethlisberger: A Rookie Sensation

    When it comes to rookie quarterbacks making an immediate impact, Ben Roethlisberger’s name is at the top of the list. In 2004, Roethlisberger burst onto the scene as the starting quarterback for the Pittsburgh Steelers and led them to a remarkable 15-1 record in the regular season.

    Roethlisberger’s performance during his rookie year was nothing short of sensational. He showcased his remarkable talent, decision-making, and poise under pressure, earning him the respect of his teammates, coaches, and fans alike. His ability to extend plays and find open receivers downfield made him a threat to any defense.

    But it was in the playoffs where Roethlisberger truly solidified his status as a star quarterback. He led the Steelers to the AFC Championship game, displaying his clutch performances and composure in high-pressure situations. Although they fell just short of a Super Bowl appearance that year, Roethlisberger’s rookie campaign set the stage for what was to come.

    In the years that followed, Roethlisberger continued to shine. He guided the Steelers to two Super Bowl victories, in Super Bowl XL and Super Bowl XLIII, cementing his place among the elite quarterbacks in NFL history. Roethlisberger’s ability to rise to the occasion in crucial moments and deliver game-changing plays made him a fan favorite and a nightmare for opposing defenses.

    Throughout his career, Roethlisberger has been a pillar of stability and leadership for the Pittsburgh Steelers. His dedication to his craft and his team has earned him respect both on and off the field. Despite facing numerous challenges and setbacks, Roethlisberger has consistently displayed his resilience and determination to succeed.

    As one of the most accomplished quarterbacks in the history of the Pittsburgh Steelers, Roethlisberger’s impact on the franchise cannot be overstated. He has become synonymous with success, leading the team to multiple playoff appearances and Super Bowl runs.

    Ben Roethlisberger’s rookie season was just the beginning of an illustrious career that has seen him achieve greatness on the field. His Super Bowl wins, clutch performances, and unwavering dedication to the Pittsburgh Steelers have made him a beloved figure among fans and a respected opponent for rival teams.

    Terry Bradshaw: A Super Bowl Winner

    Terry Bradshaw, the legendary quarterback of the Pittsburgh Steelers, is celebrated for his remarkable success in the Super Bowl, boasting a flawless 4-0 record in the biggest game of the NFL season. Bradshaw played a pivotal role in leading the dominant Steelers teams of the 1970s to greatness and solidified his place among the greatest quarterbacks in NFL history.

    Bradshaw’s ability to throw the deep ball struck fear into opposing defenses, constantly stretching the field and keeping opponents on their toes. His leadership skills were unparalleled, guiding the Steelers through their Super Bowl triumphs and garnering recognition as the Super Bowl MVP.

    “Terry Bradshaw’s talent and leadership were truly remarkable. He was the heart and soul of the Steelers teams that dominated the 1970s,” said Coach Chuck Noll.

    Bradshaw’s exceptional career led him to be inducted into the Pro Football Hall of Fame, cementing his legacy as one of the all-time greats. His Super Bowl victories and MVP honors are testaments to his extraordinary skills and contributions to the game.

    Troy Aikman: Leading the Cowboys to Success

    Troy Aikman, the legendary quarterback for the Dallas Cowboys, played a pivotal role in the team’s dynasty during the 1990s. Aikman’s leadership and on-field prowess were crucial in establishing the Cowboys as a dominant force in the NFL.

    Under Aikman’s guidance, the Dallas Cowboys won three Super Bowl titles in just four years (1992, 1993, and 1995), solidifying their status as one of the most successful franchises in NFL history. Aikman’s ability to make accurate passes and push the ball downfield opened up opportunities for the team’s star players, enabling the Cowboys to showcase their offensive prowess.

    Known for his exceptional poise and decision-making skills, Aikman commanded the Cowboys’ offense with precision and finesse. His leadership both on and off the field was instrumental in creating a cohesive and winning culture within the team.

    Throughout his career, Aikman completed over 61% of his passes for over 32,000 yards and 165 touchdowns. His impressive numbers, combined with his Super Bowl success, earned him a well-deserved place in the Pro Football Hall of Fame.

    While his individual statistics may not rival some of the other quarterbacks on this list, Aikman’s impact on the game cannot be overstated. His leadership, determination, and ability to perform under pressure were instrumental in the Dallas Cowboys’ success during the 1990s.

    Overall, Troy Aikman’s contributions as the quarterback for the Dallas Cowboys were instrumental in their Super Bowl triumphs and their status as one of the greatest teams in NFL history.

    Bart Starr: Efficient and Effective

    Bart Starr, the legendary quarterback for the Green Bay Packers, played a pivotal role in the team’s success during the 1960s. While Vince Lombardi receives much of the attention, it was Starr who led the Packers’ offense and made things happen on the field.

    Starr was known for his solid and efficient passing game, which contributed to the Packers winning multiple championships. His precision and accuracy as a passer allowed the team to consistently move the ball down the field and put points on the board.

    In particular, Starr’s leadership qualities and ability to make crucial plays in pressure situations were instrumental to the Packers’ success. He led the team to victory in the first two Super Bowls, winning the Most Valuable Player (MVP) award in 1966.

    “I firmly believe that Starr is the best quarterback who ever played the game.” – Vince Lombardi

    Under Starr’s guidance, the Packers became a dominant force in the NFL, winning five NFL championships in the 1960s. His partnership with coach Vince Lombardi created a winning culture in Green Bay that remains a part of the team’s legacy to this day.

    Starr’s contributions on the field were recognized not only by his teammates and coaches but also by the league. In addition to his championships and MVP award, he was a four-time Pro Bowler and a member of the NFL 1960s All-Decade Team.

    Roger Staubach: A Slice of History

    Roger Staubach holds a prominent place in the annals of NFL history as the legendary quarterback for the Dallas Cowboys. Staubach’s illustrious career saw him lead the Cowboys to two Super Bowl titles in the 1970s, solidifying his status as a true icon of the game.

    Throughout his career, Staubach’s exceptional talent and leadership abilities were evident, earning him numerous accolades. He was selected to the Pro Bowl six times, showcasing his exceptional skills and impact on the field. Staubach’s ability to make plays with his legs, allowing him to scramble and extend plays, set him apart from other quarterbacks of his era.

    Staubach’s impact on the Dallas Cowboys and the NFL extended far beyond his impressive statistics and Super Bowl victories. Despite not becoming a full-time starter until age 29 due to his military service, Staubach’s determination and tenacity made him a force to be reckoned with. His contributions to the Cowboys’ success and his ability to rally his team in critical moments left a lasting legacy in the franchise’s storied history.

    “Roger Staubach was more than just a quarterback. He was a leader, a game-changer, and his passion for the game was unmatched. He had a knack for making plays when it mattered most, earning him the nickname ‘Captain Comeback.’ Staubach’s impact on the Dallas Cowboys will forever be etched in NFL history.”

    Otto Graham: The Dominant Quarterback

    Otto Graham was an exceptional quarterback who played for the Cleveland Browns from 1946 to 1955. Throughout his illustrious career in both the All-America Football Conference (AAFC) and the National Football League (NFL), Graham showcased his dominance on the field.

    The Cleveland Browns experienced tremendous success under Graham’s leadership, capturing a remarkable four AAFC titles and three NFL titles. Graham’s ability to lead his team to championships solidified his status as one of the greatest quarterbacks in NFL history.

    Not only was Graham successful in terms of championships, but he also held numerous passing records during his career. His unparalleled efficiency and exceptional leadership paved the way for his team’s success. Graham’s impact on the game was truly ahead of his time, leaving an indelible mark on the sport.

    FAQ

    Who is considered the best quarterback in NFL history?

    The debate over who is the greatest NFL quarterback of all time is ongoing. While opinions may vary, some of the top contenders often mentioned include Joe Montana, Tom Brady, Peyton Manning, and Johnny Unitas.

    Who are some of the greatest quarterbacks of all time?

    Some of the greatest quarterbacks in NFL history include Joe Montana, Tom Brady, Peyton Manning, Johnny Unitas, Brett Favre, Joe Namath, Dan Marino, Steve Young, Aaron Rodgers, and Drew Brees.

    What makes a quarterback legendary in the NFL?

    Legendary NFL quarterbacks are often defined by their achievements, records, impact on the game, leadership skills, and ability to perform under pressure. They have left a lasting legacy and have had a significant influence on the sport.

    How do achievements and records contribute to a quarterback’s legacy?

    Achievements and records serve as tangible evidence of a quarterback’s success and skill.     Tom Brady currently holds the record for the most Super Bowl wins by a quarterback, with seven championships to his name as of 2021.

    Who is the youngest quarterback to win a Super Bowl?

    What role does leadership play in a quarterback’s legacy?

    Leadership is a crucial aspect of a quarterback’s legacy. Great quarterbacks are often seen as the leaders of their teams, guiding and inspiring their teammates to perform at their best. Their ability to lead both on and off the field can have a significant impact on the success of their team.

    How does a quarterback’s impact on the game contribute to their status as one of the greatest?

    A quarterback’s impact on the game can be measured by their ability to revolutionize the position, influence the strategies used by teams, and leave a lasting mark on the sport. Quarterbacks who introduce new techniques or change the way the game is played often find themselves among the greatest in NFL history.
