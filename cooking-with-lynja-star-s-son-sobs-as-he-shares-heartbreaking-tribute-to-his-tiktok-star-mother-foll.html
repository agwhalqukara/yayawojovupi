Cooking with Lynja star's son sobs as he shares heartbreaking tribute to his TikTok star mother foll

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, July 20, 2024

The son of the now-deceased elderly social media influencer chef who inspired millions with her cooking videos shared a heartbreaking tribute. 

Lynn Yamada Davis died on January 1 at Riverview Medical Center in New Jersey from esophageal cancer, her daughter, Hannah Mariko Shofet, said.

Her son, Tim Davis, posted a teary-eyed video of himself on her TikTok page today, showing pictures of the deceased creator. 

The video is captioned: ‘You’re the best mom I could have ever asked for but an even better friend. I love you mom.’

Tim showed childhood pictures of his mom and a photo of her when she was a high school senior. 

He said: ‘She has the Cornell hoodie on but she ended up going to MIT.’

He also showed various photos of his mother in college and remarked that she looked like a ‘model.’

Tim even showed pictures of Lynn with his other brother, Sean and photos of himself as a baby with her.

The family has kept her social media page active after her death because she asked for clips that have already been edited to be posted. 

Cooking With Lynja grew in popularity following a video of her showing off some quirky dance moves as she made a bacon, egg and cheese sandwich and she currently has 17.4 million followers. 

‘My mom was like my partner in crime,’ Tim, who edits the TikTok account, told The New York Times. 

He said his mother asked him to post the funny videos they made together around 10 years ago. But after he uploads the last clip that involves Lynn, the account will become inactive. 

Lynn would inject fun energy into her cooking videos, such as her dancing, which appealed to the younger generations. 

She became a huge success on social media with her wholesome style and unique personality. Forbes included her on its 50 over 50 list in 2022. 

Lynn was born in New York City but lived in Fort Lee, New Jersey, during her early years with her businessman father Tadao Yamada and homemaker mother Mabel Fujisake Yamada.

She graduated from MIT in 1977 with a civil engineering bachelor’s degree before earning a master’s in business administration and public health from Colombia. 

The mother had worked for Bell Labs, which is now AT&T Labs, and enjoyed a long telecommunications career before she discovered TikTok fame.
